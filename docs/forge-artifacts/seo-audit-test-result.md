## Layer 2 — Editorial Quality

### E1: Hook Quality — Strong (3)

**Evidence:** "Formex visade i januari riktningen för nordisk inredning under kommande säsonger, och ett mönster var tydligare än de flesta: naturliga fibrer dominerar textilkategorierna på ett sätt som inte synts sedan sjuttiotalet."

**Diagnosis:** Opens with a specific named event (Formex), a specific time (januari), and a counter-intuitive historical comparison ("inte synts sedan sjuttiotalet") — not interchangeable with any article on this topic.

**Fix:** No action required. The historical comparison creates immediate curiosity and the Pantone/Mocha Mousse detail in the same paragraph adds dateable specificity that passes the specialist test.

---

### E2: Thesis Clarity — Adequate (2)

**Argument summary:** "The natural-fiber trend now treated as a semantic statement — the rug communicates a home's values — has transformed it from the last purchase into the primary tone-setter, and breadth of sortiment makes this shift accessible."

**Diagnosis:** The thesis IS present and extractable, but it arrives in full only in P5. The middle section (P3 — practical sizing/color guidance) neither underpins nor deepens it — it surveys practical rug knowledge without connecting to the cultural/values argument. An article that argues "material is a statement" should show why size and color choices are *part of that statement*, not list them as separate practical facts.

**Fix:** In P3, reframe the practical content to serve the thesis explicitly. Instead of "En generös matta i formatet 200x300 centimeter skapar en visuell grund som samlar möblerna i vardagsrummet" (encyclopedia), frame it as: why the size decision *is* the statement. Example direction: "Inredningsarkitekten som väljer en 200x300 jutematta till ett öppet vardagsrum gör inte ett inredningsval — hon gör ett rumsanspråk. Storleken avgör om materialet dominerar eller nöjer sig med att vara ett tillägg." One sentence like this ties P3's practical content to the thesis rather than suspending it.

### E4: Trustlink Integration — Adequate (2)

**Trustlink 1:** "Enligt en kartläggning av textiltrenderna för 2025 är preferensen för naturliga fibrer som lin, hampa och ull en av årets starkaste rörelser, driven av en bransch som integrerar digitala produktpass för full transparens kring materialens ursprung."
→ Diagnosis: Anchor text is descriptive ("en kartläggning av textiltrenderna för 2025") — strong. The claim introduced ("en av årets starkaste rörelser") is specific in direction but not quantified. The "digitala produktpass" detail is concrete. However, the sourced section runs for two full sentences without a specific number, percentage, or named actor from the source. Compare to job_08 standard: "50% of workday locating info, 7.5% docs lost" — a reader-memorable statistic.

**Fix:** Add one specific number from the fabricoeur.se source. If the source contains percentage data on natural fiber preference growth, a specific count of brands adopting digital product passports, or a measurable market claim, surface it in this sentence: "driven av en bransch som — [specific stat] — integrerar digitala produktpass..." Even an approximate ("mer än hälften av de nordiska textilmärkena som ställde ut på Formex") would elevate this from trend-claim to fact-claim.

### E5: Anchor Naturalness — Strong (3)

**Anchor sentence:** "Ett sortiment med 240 mattor i olika material, storlekar och prisklasser innebär att valet mellan en handknuten ullmatta och en lättsköttare sisalmatta inte längre kräver specialistbutiker eller långa leveranstider."

**Remove-link test:** "Ett sortiment med 240 mattor i olika material, storlekar och prisklasser innebär att valet mellan en handknuten ullmatta och en lättsköttare sisalmatta inte längre kräver specialistbutiker eller långa leveranstider." — Sentence works perfectly standalone. The number 240 creates specificity, the handknuten/lättsköttare contrast creates a real purchase tension.

**Diagnosis:** P4 is the most concrete and specific paragraph in the article. It answers the practical question that P3 raised (which material for which room?) by pointing to breadth of available choice. The anchor lands as the natural practical destination of a cultural/material argument. SERP entities (jutemattor, ryamattor, sisalmatta, ullmatta, gångmatta, fårskinn) are dense in this paragraph.

### E6: Red Thread — Adequate (2)

**Weakest transition:**
- Close of P2: "...egenskaper som gör att en handknuten ullmatta kan hålla i decennier utan att belasta vare sig hemmiljön eller miljön i stort."
- Open of P3: "I praktiken handlar valet av matta om mer än fiber."
- Issue: P2 ends deep in sustainability and material properties of wool. P3 opens with "but there's more than fiber" — the "mer än fiber" pivot after a paragraph about fiber is abrupt rather than built from P2's ending. The transition feels like a gear-change rather than a deepening.

**Thread summary:** The overall argument develops: P1 (cultural shift) → P2 (sustainability depth of the shift) → P3 (practical dimension of choice) → P4 (accessibility of choice) → P5 (why this matters culturally). Tracing is possible, but P2→P3 is the weakest joint. P4→P5 works because P5 picks up the Formex frame from P1, closing a rhetorical loop. P3's practical content is the article's most detachable section — it could be moved or significantly reduced without breaking the cultural argument.

**Fix:** Rewrite P2's final sentence to plant the practical question P3 will answer. Instead of ending on "hemmiljön eller miljön i stort" (environment/sustainability), end with something that opens the practical dimension naturally: "...biologiskt nedbrytbar, egenskaper som gör att valet av fiber inte bara är ett hållbarhetsbeslut utan det praktiska beslut som sedan avgör om mattan faktiskt fungerar i det rum den hamnar." P3 then picks up "fungerar i det rum" naturally.

### E7: Closing Quality — Strong (3)

**Final sentence:** "Mattan är inte längre den sista pusselbiten som köps på väg ut från möbelvaruhuset utan den första textilen som sätter rummets ton, den yta under fötterna som avgör om hemmet känns genomtänkt eller tillfälligt."

**Diagnosis:** Strong. P5 introduces genuinely new content — the semiotic argument that fiber choice *communicates values* ("Jutens grova textur talar ett annat språk än viskosens glans"). The final sentence is a reframing, not a recap: it repositions the rug from "last purchase" to "first textile" — a claim that cannot be made in any other article, as it depends on everything argued before it. "Den yta under fötterna som avgör om hemmet känns genomtänkt eller tillfälligt" stays with the reader.

**Diagnosis:** The article does not smell AI-generated at the sentence level — vocabulary is varied, entities are deployed naturally, and the hook/closing are genuinely specific. The material-function pairings (sisal for hall, viskos for comfort) can be compressed to one sentence. (2) Allow one short paragraph (50-70 words) somewhere — P3's opening ("I praktiken handlar valet av matta om mer än fiber.") could stand alone as a transitional pivot rather than being absorbed into a 161-word block.
