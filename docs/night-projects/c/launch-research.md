## 1. Product Launch Checklist — What Deliverables Are Needed

A complete launch spans five areas. Most failures trace to skipping one of them.

### 1.1 Product Readiness (must complete before public activity)
- Engineering sign-off: QA complete, no critical bugs
- UX/UI review: design approved
- Legal/compliance sign-off
- Operations sign-off on support capacity
- Security review

### 1.2 Go-to-Market
- Positioning statement (who, what problem, why better)
- Messaging framework (core value prop + 3 supporting proof points)
- Pricing finalized
- Press/media kit: press release draft, product screenshots, founder photos, one-pager
- Distribution channels confirmed
- Analyst/influencer briefings scheduled

### 1.3 Marketing Deliverables
- Landing page live and tested
- Launch email sequence written and scheduled
- Social media content calendar (minimum 4 weeks)
- Blog post / announcement copy
- Demo video (1–3 minutes)
- Ad creative (if paid channels)
- Product Hunt profile set up

### 1.4 Sales & Support
- Sales pitch deck updated
- Demo script written
- Competitive battle cards
- FAQ / objection-handling guide
- Support team briefed
- Customer success scripts ready

### 1.5 Post-Launch
- KPI dashboard set up pre-launch (capture day-0 data)
- Feedback collection mechanism (in-app, survey)
- Retro scheduled for T+7 and T+30
- Follow-up email drafted (for trial users, leads, press)

## 2. YC Demo Day Pitch Deck — The Structure

YC coaches a 2-minute 15-second presentation. 10–15 slides, strict narrative arc.

### Slide-by-slide (with timing)

| # | Slide | Content | Time |
|---|-------|---------|------|
| 1 | **Title** | Company name + one-line punchline | 5 sec |
| 2 | **Problem** | Who hurts & how much (quantified) | 10 sec |
| 3 | **Customer Quote** | Real pain in their own words | 5 sec |
| 4 | **Solution** | One-sentence product promise | 10 sec |
| 5 | **Demo** | 3-step screen recording, no voiceover | 15 sec |
| 6 | **Market Size** | TAM >$1B with named source | 10 sec |
| 7 | **Competition** | 2-axis quadrant, you in top-right | 5 sec |
| 8 | **Why Now** | Tech or regulatory tailwind | 5 sec |
| 9 | **Traction** | Revenue, users, MoM growth rate | 10 sec |
| 10 | **Business Model** | Who pays, how much, gross margin | 5 sec |
| 11 | **Go-to-Market** | The compounding acquisition loop | 5 sec |
| 12 | **Team** | One photo + one past win per person | 5 sec |
| 13 | **Ask** | Raising $X for Y milestones | 5 sec |
| 14 | **Vision** | 10-word world-domination line | 5 sec |
| 15 | **Thank You** | QR code to calendar / contact | 5 sec |

### Design rules
- One idea per slide, large font, white background
- Visually boring is intentional — clarity over design
- No animation, minimal color
- The main point must be understood at a glance

### Narrative arc
Problem → Customer validates pain → Solution → Proof (demo) → Market exists → You're positioned to win → Team can execute → Give us money

## 3. Product Hunt Launch Strategy

### Pre-launch (4–6 weeks out)
- Build community presence: upvote others, leave real comments daily for weeks before launch
- Secure a **top-500 hunter** — posts by top hunters get 3.2x more upvotes
- Build a Product Hunt notification list
- Prepare maker profile: photo, bio, previous products

### Launch mechanics
- **Launch at 12:01 AM PST** to maximize the full 24-hour voting window
- **Best days**: Tuesday–Thursday for B2B (weekday = more traffic; weekend = less competition)
- All top-7 products in 2025 had demo videos — founder-narrated screen recordings outperform polished agency videos
- Write a genuine first-person intro as the first comment on your post

### Launch day execution
- **First 3 hours are decisive** — early engagement signals algorithm priority
- Focus energy on hours 0–4 and hours 22–24
- Activate personal network manually: direct messages > mass emails
- Reply to every comment within minutes
- Do not buy upvotes — algorithm detects and deprioritizes

### What drives rankings
1. Upvote velocity in first 3 hours
2. Comment volume and quality
3. Hunter's reputation
4. Video quality (authentic > polished)

### Realistic outcomes
- Conversion rate from PH visitors: 1–3%
- Best value: credibility signal + community feedback + PR hook
- Not a primary growth channel — a brand and credibility lever

## 4. SaaS Launch Email Sequence — 5-Email Pre-Launch Drip

Start 2–3 weeks before launch. Drip to build anticipation without full reveal.

### Email 1 — The Teaser (T-14 days)
- **Subject**: "Something's coming" / "Keep your eyes open" / "Are you ready for this?"
- **Content**: vague hint at a game-changing solution, no product name yet. Focus on the problem. End with "Stay tuned — more soon."
- **Goal**: open curiosity loop

### Email 2 — Problem Agitation (T-10 days)
- **Subject**: "The [pain point] problem is worse than you think"
- **Content**: tell a specific story about the problem. Use a customer voice or a data point. Hint that you've been building a solution. Include one teaser image or blurred screenshot.
- **Goal**: build relatability and urgency around the problem

### Email 3 — The Reveal (T-7 days)
- **Subject**: "Here's what we've been building" / "Introducing [Product Name]" / "[Product Name] is almost here"
- **Content**: name the product, show what it does in 1–2 sentences, include a demo GIF or short video. Open waitlist or early access if not already done.
- **Goal**: convert curiosity to intent

### Email 4 — Social Proof / Scarcity (T-3 days)
- **Subject**: "What [beta tester] said about [Product]" / "We're almost full on early access"
- **Content**: beta user testimonial or quote, unique value prop reinforced, limited early-bird pricing or spots. CTA = reserve your spot.
- **Goal**: reduce risk perception, create scarcity

### Email 5 — Launch Day (T-0)
- **Subject**: "We're live" / "[Product] is officially open" / "Today's the day"
- **Content**: it's live announcement, primary CTA button, brief value prop recap, early-bird offer with 48–72 hour expiry. Optional: personal founder note.
- **Goal**: convert

### Tactics that work
- Scarcity + urgency combined converts 2–3x better than either alone
- Subject lines under 40 characters outperform longer ones
- Outcome language in CTAs lifts clicks ~150 basis points ("Get your first [X]" vs "Sign up")

## 5. Landing Page Copy Framework

### Section 1 — Hero (above the fold)

**Formula**: `[Outcome] for [Audience] without [Obstacle]`

Components (in order):
1. **Headline**: Single biggest benefit, one punchy sentence. Not what it is — what it does for them.
2. **Sub-headline**: One sentence expanding with specificity. Add a number or outcome.
3. **Social proof hook**: Small element in immediate view — "Trusted by 4,200+ teams" or star rating
4. **CTA button**: Outcome language, action verb. "Start for free," "Get your first report," "See it in action"
5. **Hero image / demo**: Product in use, not a logo or abstract visual

**Rule**: Main point understood in under 5 seconds.

### Section 2 — Problem / Context
- Articulate the pain before presenting the solution
- Use language your customer uses (mirror their words from reviews/interviews)
- 2–3 sentences max

### Section 3 — Features as Benefits
- 3-column format: icon + feature name + 1-line benefit statement
- Lead with the benefit ("Never miss a deadline" not "Smart reminders")
- 3–6 features max

### Section 4 — Social Proof
- Testimonials with full name, title, company, photo
- Logos of recognizable customers (even 3–5 matters)
- Numbers: "X customers," "Y% faster," "$Z saved per month"
- Video testimonials outperform text 2:1

### Section 5 — How It Works
- 3-step numbered sequence
- Makes the path from signup to value feel short
- Optional: short demo video embedded here

### Section 6 — Objection Handling / FAQ
- Answer the 3–5 most common blockers (pricing, security/trust, "is this right for me")

### Section 7 — Final CTA
- Repeat the hero CTA
- Risk reducer: "No credit card required," "Cancel anytime," "Free 14-day trial"
- Urgency if available: "Join 500+ teams already using [X]"

### Copy formulas
- **Headline**: `[Verb] [Outcome] [Time Frame or Qualifier]` → "Cut your support tickets in half, automatically"
- **CTA**: Outcome verb + what they receive + optional qualifier → "Get your first report free"
- **Social proof**: Specific outcome + named person + recognizable company

## 6. How Stripe, Linear, and Vercel Actually Launched

### Stripe
**Core insight**: Make it so easy developers can't not use it.

- Launched into YC network — first 10–30 customers came directly from YC
- Built the product to be integrable in 7 lines of code (the demo was the pitch)
- 1,000+ developers on waitlist before public launch — all word of mouth
- Patrick Collison configured the system to email founders on every single API error — treated every error as a product failure
- Public chat room for support so founders could see integration problems in real time
- Lifetime marketing spend: $0. Distribution came through developer blogs and community.
- **Strategy**: nail the developer experience, let developers tell other developers

### Linear
**Core insight**: Announce before you have a product. Keep announcing.

- Announced the company before the product was built → generated early waitlist
- Launched again at seed funding → reached more people
- Launched again when product opened to everyone with pricing → new segment
- Launched again at Series A → each launch compounded on the previous
- Each "level up" took ~6 months: major release + dedicated messaging + standalone landing page
- Lifetime paid ad spend: $35,000 (essentially zero)
- Growth channel: product quality + word of mouth within the developer/maker community
- Dominated one segment (early-stage startups) before expanding — "Slack Curve"
- **Strategy**: "Launch and keep launching" (linear.app/method/launching) — shipping is marketing

### Vercel
**Core insight**: Never sell. Make trying irresistible. Expand through team growth.

- Built Next.js (open source) before Vercel (commercial) — built the community before the product
- Self-serve first: deploy in minutes, no demo call required
- Free tier as trust mechanism: developers validate before committing
- Tracked two growth signals: (1) account goes from solo → 2+ developers, (2) grows beyond one team — these triggered expansion conversations
- Customer success staffed with senior engineers who could read customer code and debug directly — not link to docs
- Focused on two verticals first: media/publishing and e-commerce — where Next.js value was most obvious
- Technical Account Executives who demoed via git repos and starter kits, not slide decks
- Grew to $200M+ ARR without traditional outbound — product-led, community-led
- **Strategy**: open source → free tier → individual developer → team → enterprise

### Common pattern across all three
- Zero or near-zero paid acquisition
- Developer community trust as distribution
- Product quality was the marketing — each made something developers wanted to share
- Self-serve onboarding that removed all friction to first value

## 7. Social Media Launch Calendar Template

### Phase timeline

| Phase | Timing | Cadence |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Foundation | T-6 to T-4 weeks | 1–2x/week |
| Awareness | T-4 to T-2 weeks | 2–3x/week |
| Pre-launch heat | T-2 weeks to T-1 day | 3–4x/week |
| Launch week | Launch day + 7 days | Daily (min 1x) |
| Post-launch | T+7 to T+30 | 2–3x/week |

### Week-by-week content plan

**Weeks 5–6 out (Foundation)**
- Behind-the-scenes: "We've been building something..."
- Problem post: surface the pain point without mentioning the product
- Founder story: why this problem matters personally

**Weeks 3–4 out (Awareness)**
- Teaser content: blurred screenshots, "coming soon" visuals
- Problem-focused educational posts (builds ICP relevance)
- Community engagement: reply to conversations in your space
- Waitlist / notify-me CTA starts appearing in bio and posts

**Weeks 1–2 out (Heat)**
- Reveal content: name the product, show the UI
- "X days until launch" countdown content
- Beta tester testimonial posts
- LinkedIn carousel (5 slides, educational)
- Twitter/X thread (7 tweets, behind-the-scenes)
- Video: 60-second walkthrough posted natively on each platform

**Launch day**
- Announcement post across all channels (customize per platform, no identical cross-posts)
- Product Hunt post link (if launching there)
- Founder personal post — story-driven, personal voice
- Reply to every comment same day

**T+1 to T+7**
- "Thank you" post: signal the community reception
- Press mention amplification
- Early results: "X people signed up in 24 hours"
- FAQ post: answer the top question you've received

**T+8 to T+30**
- Case study / early customer story
- Feature spotlight posts (one per week)
- Social proof: repost user mentions
- "What we learned" transparency post (builds trust and drives engagement)

### Platform-specific rules

| Platform | Content type | Format | Length |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| Twitter/X | Hot take, thread, founder voice | Short punchy + 7-tweet threads | Tweet: <280, thread: 7 tweets |
| LinkedIn | Educational, data-backed | Carousel (5 slides) or text post | 150–300 words |
| Instagram | Visual, behind-the-scenes | Reels (30–60s), carousel | Caption: 100–150 words |
| YouTube | Long-form demo, story | 3–10 min | Full walkthrough |

### Content mix rule (70/20/10)
- 70% value content (educational, useful, entertaining)
- 20% curated / shared external content
- 10% promotional (direct product mention)

## Key Pattern Across All Topics

Every successful launch framework converges on the same logic:

1. **Start with the problem, not the product** — hero copy, pitch decks, email sequences all open on pain
2. **Drip, don't dump** — tease over weeks, not days; linear reveals build anticipation
3. **Social proof precedes the ask** — proof before CTA, always
4. **Launch is not a moment, it's a campaign** — Stripe, Linear, Vercel all launched repeatedly
5. **Community trust compounds** — the best launches (Linear: $35k lifetime ad spend, Stripe: $0) were built on product quality, not advertising budget
